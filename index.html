Promoting STEM Eudcation

STEM Performance Crisis

Texas students are experiencing critically low academic performance in STEM subjects, with 55% below grade level in math and declining standardized test scores, threatening future educational and economic competitiveness.

1. Focus on enhancing the math and science curriculum to ensure students have a strong foundation in these critical subject

2. Follow a more project-based approach to learning, allowing students to apply theoretical knowledge to real-world scenarios

3. Incorporate STEM principles into various projects and activities to foster a deeper understanding and appreciation of these fields

Academic Deficit

The United States lags behind Asian countries in academic performance, with global test scores revealing significant weaknesses in Math, Science, and Reading proficiency.

1. Implement a specialized curriculum in highschool to target student's interest in a certain profession.

2. Schools and classes customize and make their own curriculum and activites to personalize to each student.

3. Encourage collaboration with local industries to provide real-world STEM experiences and mentorship opportunities.

Insuffiecient Resources in STEM

Lack of STEM Resources and Opportunities

Increasing funding can help provide for more resources. Implementing a project-based curriculum helps build more hands-on STEM environment. Partnering with companies will increase learning opportunities.

Decline in Student Engagement in STEM

Create and join extracurriculars and participate in competitions and fun opportunities/activities to help develop STEM skills. Doing this with friends can increase motivation and engagement while still learning.
